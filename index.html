The Ideas Behind Poetry

"There are three things, after all, that a poem must reach: the eye, the ear, and what we may call the heart or the mind. It is most important of all to reach the heart of the reader."

"Poetry is the spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings: it takes its origin from emotion recollected in tranquility."

Emotions and Poetry

Feelings are often hard to put into words and once they are words, those receiving them can process those feelings within themselves. Poetry is a connection between the mind and outpouring of the author and a willing reader. Poetry holds the key to something that seems so inexpressible. When a poet puts his pen to the paper, he seeks to capture the eye, inviting the ear to hear, and reaching out to the mind to feel and understand the depths of pain or heights of joy the poet is in.

The Crafting of Poetry Through Recollective Tranquility

Poetry indeed does originate from recollective tranquility. An example is in the poem, “Bells For John Whiteside's Daughter”. It seems so difficult to get anything out of this poem. But with a closer examination into the title, it is clear that there are two reasons for bells to someone, and those being death or marriage. Understanding this, the poem completely transfigures into a new literary masterpiece. John Crowe Ransom manifests the overwhelming feelings of loss of a loved one as he writes, “But now go the bells, and we are ready, In one house we are sternly stopped To say we are vexed at her brown study, Lying so primly propped.” Understanding this poem for me has brought back wave after wave of emotion as I think of the great losses my family has suffered as a result of death. It is difficult to explain the feeling of standing before a casket containing a body that only a few days before was filled with life. The way John Crowe expresses this impacts readers. Those who are fortunate enough to have not suffered such losses can sympathize with Crowe in the anguish he describes. Poetry has reached the minds of many peoples regardless of background. As it grows to be ever more apparent, it exponentially grows deeper into philosophies, ideas, and emotions. This is illustrated through the now infamous poem, “The New Colossus”. Poems like this go much deeper than the vast expanse of change between the ancient and the modern, the old and the new, what was and what now is. They go beyond that of mere words that may be taken with a light heart. Poetry often weighs and unexpectedly convicts an ataraxic mind. Versatile is a substantial way to describe it as it can be used to express love or cry out in protest.

The Mighty Impact of Poetry

That said, poetry has truly shaped society through the spontaneity of powerful words and ideas. What seemed unattainable years ago has become a reality today. Rockets, planes, lunar landings, all have changed life as we know it. One thing, however, that will not or cannot change, is the integrity of great poems. They have changed society by encouraging thinkers to think farther, to look beyond the state of the world, and express it into a few seemingly simple lines.
